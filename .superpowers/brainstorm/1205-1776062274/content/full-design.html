Hero
Dim/bright type contrast — filler words ("Your", "should be") fade to 22% opacity so "closing deals." hits like a title card. Subtle grid texture + atmospheric red/cobalt light instead of floating orbs. Scroll indicator on the right edge.
Problem
Numbered list replaces identical glass cards — horizontal rule separators, cobalt numbers, no glassmorphism. More scannable and editorial. Cobalt orb stays as atmosphere (on-brand vs purple which is removed).
What We Do
Service cards with amber numbering — glass cards simplified to flat bordered rows with amber numbered labels instead of just titles. Photo background stays but pattern texture replaces it in the mockup.
How It Works
Vertical timeline with red thread — red connecting line with glowing dots replaces isolated 3-column cards. Creates visual flow and momentum. Much more cinematic than the current grid.
Stats (was: Social Proof)
Red section now earns its place — "27yr / DC / $0 / 2" in oversized type on red background with grain overlay. Replaces the empty commented-out testimonial block. Credibility before the founders section.
Founders
Photos bleed edge-to-edge in 3:4 cards — name + role overlay at the bottom of each photo like a film credit. More cinematic than a square thumbnail in a glass card. Amber orb provides warmth.
FAQ
Cream section break — the only cream section on the page. Creates a dark → dark → dark → red → dark → cream → dark rhythm that makes every section feel distinct. Questions on cream feel trustworthy and open.
Final CTA
Dim/bright technique mirrors the hero — filler words dimmed, key phrase bright. Cobalt bleeds in from the left, red from the right. Creates a bookend feel with the hero.
Removed
All purple orbs eliminated — purple (#8B5CF6) never appeared in the brand palette. Replaced everywhere with amber (#C17F3A) for warmth or cobalt (#1B44D8) for atmosphere. The glassmorphism "glass-card" pattern is broken up — each section now has its own visual language.
